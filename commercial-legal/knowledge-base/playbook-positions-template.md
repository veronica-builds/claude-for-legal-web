| Consequential damages | Mutual exclusion of indirect, incidental, special, exemplary, and punitive damages |

## Indemnification

| Position | Terms |
|---|---|
| Standard | Mutual indemnification for: (1) IP infringement, (2) data breaches caused by the indemnifying party, (3) gross negligence or willful misconduct, (4) bodily injury |
| Acceptable | Unilateral IP indemnification from the vendor if the vendor supplies the primary technology |
| Escalation (RED) | Unilateral indemnification for "any breach"; uncapped indemnification without carveout back to the liability cap; IP indemnification that covers both parties' pre-existing IP |
| Procedure | Prompt notice; right to control defense; right to approve settlement (not to be unreasonably withheld); indemnitee cooperation obligation |

## Intellectual Property

| Position | Terms |
|---|---|
| Pre-existing IP | Each party retains all rights to pre-existing IP; no assignment |
| Developed IP — services | IP developed specifically for us and paid for by us vests in us; general enhancements to the vendor's platform remain the vendor's |
| Developed IP — SaaS | We receive a license to use; vendor retains ownership |
| License grant to us | Non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free right to use the vendor's technology for our internal business purposes |
| Feedback clause | Acceptable only if limited to non-confidential feedback and does not include a license to our pre-existing IP or deliverables |
| Escalation (RED) | IP assignment of our pre-existing IP; work-for-hire clause extending beyond clearly defined deliverables; unrestricted feedback clause granting perpetual irrevocable license |

## Data Protection

| Position | Terms |
|---|---|
| DPA required | Yes — for any vendor that processes, stores, or has access to personal data of our clients, employees, or users |
| Controller/processor classification | We are controller; vendor is processor for personal data processed on our behalf |
| Sub-processor authorization | General authorization acceptable IF vendor provides prior notice (minimum 10 business days) and we have right to object |
| Breach notification | Vendor must notify us within 24–48 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach (to enable our 72-hour regulatory obligation) |
| Data deletion | Within 30–60 days of contract termination; written confirmation of deletion |
| Audit rights | Right to audit (or accept SOC 2 Type II in lieu of audit) once per year; audit upon cause without restriction |
| Cross-border transfers | Must have valid transfer mechanism (EU SCCs, adequacy decision, etc.) for data transfers from EU/UK |
| Data processing location | Must disclose all processing locations; no processing in [RESTRICTED JURISDICTIONS — fill in] without prior written consent |
| Escalation (RED) | No DPA when personal data is involved; blanket sub-processor authorization without notification; breach notification timeline > 72 hours; no cross-border transfer mechanism for EU/UK data |

## Confidentiality (Non-NDA Context)

| Position | Terms |
|---|---|
| Standard | Mutual; limited to non-public information; standard carveouts (public domain, independently developed, third-party disclosure, legal compulsion) |
| Term | Duration of agreement plus 3–5 years; trade secrets for as long as they remain trade secrets |
| Acceptable | Unilateral if we are only disclosing |
| Escalation (RED) | Perpetual confidentiality without trade secret justification; no independent development carveout |
